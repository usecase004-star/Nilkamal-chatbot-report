09 — Key Insights
📈
Massive Launch Spike — 457 Users on Day 1 (May 9)
The chatbot went from near-zero traffic to 457 users on May 9, indicating a strong WhatsApp campaign or mass broadcast trigger. Volume stabilised at 346–410/day in subsequent days, showing good retention of the channel. This is a healthy pattern — the launch worked, and users are returning or being re-engaged daily.
⚠️
33.9% Bounce Rate — 1 in 3 Users Sends Just 1 Message
1,348 users sent only a single message and dropped off. This is the chatbot equivalent of a missed call. Common triggers: users saying "hi" or "hello" and not proceeding past the service list. The welcome message and service menu needs to be more compelling — consider adding a personalised opening message or a time-limited offer to pull users deeper into the funnel.
🛍️
Product Discovery is the #1 Use Case — 4.8 Products Shown Per User
1,319 users (33.1%) used the Explore Products flow, and the bot showed an average of 4.8 product cards per product-seeker. 2,807 total product cards were shown across 584 users who actually saw products. Mattress is the dominant category (1,668 mentions) followed by Bed Frame (1,052) and Pillow (798). The product recommendation engine is clearly the most engaged feature.
📍
Store Locator Has Strong Usage but Low Booking Conversion (1.8%)
492 users triggered the store locator flow, but only 9 completed a visit booking — a 1.8% conversion rate. This gap suggests users are finding their nearest store but not committing to a time slot. The booking step (datetime selection) likely has drop-off. Consider making the time slot selection more prominent or adding a follow-up message after store address is shown to nudge the booking.
🕐
Afternoon (12–5 PM) is Peak Engagement — 1,334 Users
The highest traffic window is 12 PM – 5 PM with 1,334 users (33.5% of all traffic). The peak hour is 3 PM (300 users). Significant night usage too — 972 users between 9 PM and 6 AM — suggesting users browsing mattresses before sleep. Any broadcast messages or WhatsApp reminders should be timed for 12–3 PM for maximum open rate and engagement.
💰
Viman Nagar (Pune) is Top Booking Store — 3 of 9 Visits
Viman Nagar leads with 3 confirmed bookings, followed by Rohini Delhi and Andheri Mumbai with 2 each. Lajpat Nagar has 1 booking. This suggests strong WhatsApp user density in Pune's Viman Nagar catchment area. Consider prioritising a follow-up campaign or in-store event at Viman Nagar to capitalise on this demand concentration.
10 — Recommendations
01
Fix the 87% Complaint Form Drop-off — Add a Fallback Flow
65 out of 75 complaint users tapped "Raise Complaint" but never submitted the form. The current form likely feels too long or requires too many fields upfront. Recommended fix: reduce the complaint form to just 2 required fields — Order ID and a brief issue description — and add a WhatsApp message prompt if the user abandons the flow mid-way. A simple re-engagement message ("We noticed you started a complaint — can we help?") sent after 10 minutes of inactivity can recover a significant share of these.
02
Reduce the 33.9% Bounce Rate with a Stronger Welcome Message
1,348 users sent just one message and left. The current service list menu is generic. Replace it with a personalised, action-driving opener — e.g. "Hi! Looking for a mattress? Tell me your budget and sleep style and I'll find your perfect match in 30 seconds." A direct question pulls the user in rather than asking them to choose from a list. Also consider a time-sensitive hook like "🎯 Today's top pick under ₹15,000 — want to see it?" to drive immediate engagement.
03
Add a Time-Slot Nudge After Store Address is Shown
492 users triggered the store locator but only 9 booked a visit — a 1.8% conversion rate. The drop-off happens between seeing the store address and selecting a date-time slot. Add a more compelling CTA after the address is shown, e.g. "📅 Book your slot now and skip the queue — takes 10 seconds." Also consider sending a WhatsApp follow-up 2 hours after a store address is shared, reminding the user to complete the booking. This alone could double the booking conversion rate.
04
Prioritise Broadcast Timing for 12–3 PM Window
Afternoon 12–5 PM drives 33.5% of all traffic, with the peak at 3 PM (300 users). Any WhatsApp broadcast, campaign message, or re-engagement blast should be timed for 12 PM – 3 PM on weekdays for maximum open rate and chatbot engagement. Avoid early morning blasts — 3 AM to 6 AM sees fewer than 50 active users per hour.
05
Follow Up the 10 Complaint Form Users Within 24 Hours
The 10 users who submitted full complaint forms — including 3 delayed delivery cases, 3 defective/damaged product cases, 2 wrong product cases, and 2 30-Nights Trial queries — need a direct human follow-up within 24 hours. Especially urgent: Ujjaval (Order 13132) who explicitly said he wants to return the bed due to poor installation support, and Bharati Chogale who received the wrong product. Delays in resolving these will result in negative reviews and returns.
06
Launch a Targeted Campaign for Viman Nagar — Highest Booking Density
Viman Nagar (Pune) generated 3 of 9 confirmed store visit bookings — the highest concentration of any single store. This signals strong organic demand in that catchment area. A targeted WhatsApp broadcast to users in 411014 and nearby pin codes, combined with an in-store promotion or sleep trial event, could significantly amplify walk-ins and conversions at this location.
07
Add a "Buy Now / Add to Cart" CTA Directly in Product Cards
The bot showed 2,807 product cards to 584 users — an average of 4.8 per user. This is strong product exposure but there is no direct purchase conversion data captured. Adding a "Buy Now" or "Order on Website" button directly beneath each product card in WhatsApp would shorten the path to purchase. Users who are engaged enough to receive 5+ product recommendations are clearly in consideration mode — a direct link reduces friction and captures intent before it fades.
08
Maintain Daily Volume — The Post-Launch Plateau is Healthy
After the May 9 launch spike of 457 users, the chatbot has stabilised at 346–410 users/day — a strong, consistent baseline for a brand-new WhatsApp channel. This plateau should not be confused with decline. It is organic retention. To sustain and grow it, plan a weekly broadcast schedule with fresh content hooks — new product launches, sleep tips, mattress comparison guides — to give existing users a reason to re-engage and new users a reason to start a conversation.
